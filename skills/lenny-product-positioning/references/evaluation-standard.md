# Evaluation Layer: Product Positioning

Use this file to score the positioning draft before delivery.

## 1. Purpose

Do not treat evaluation as an appendix. Use it as a gate.
The goal is to prevent weak positioning documents from being delivered as final output.

## 2. Default threshold

- Use the user's threshold if explicitly provided.
- Otherwise default to **7/10**.

## 3. Required scoring dimensions

Score each dimension from 1 to 10.

### A. ICP clarity
- Is the target user concrete and narrow?
- Is the first wedge obvious?

### B. problem sharpness
- Is the pain real, costly, and credible?
- Is the problem described in market language rather than abstract language?

### C. alternative awareness
- Are current alternatives clearly identified?
- Is the switching reason explicit?

### D. category frame quality
- Can the market quickly understand what kind of product this is?
- Is the frame helpful rather than confusing?

### E. differentiation quality
- Is the differentiation structural, not adjective-based?
- Does the product feel meaningfully distinct?

### F. why-now strength
- Is the timing logic real and specific?
- Does the document explain why this is newly possible or urgent?

### G. narrative compression
- Is the one-line positioning memorable and reusable?
- Can someone repeat it accurately?

### H. strategic coherence
- Do wedge, category, differentiation, and expansion path fit together?

## 4. Pass / fail logic

### Pass
- overall score >= threshold
- no individual dimension below 6/10

### Conditional fail
- overall score >= threshold but one dimension < 6/10
- revise that weak dimension and re-evaluate

### Fail
- overall score < threshold
- revise and re-evaluate

## 5. Revision protocol

If the draft fails:
1. identify the weakest 2-3 dimensions
2. revise the document specifically for those weaknesses
3. re-score
4. only deliver once it passes

If it still cannot pass because inputs are missing, state:
- what information is missing
- which dimensions are blocked
- what minimum input is needed to pass

## 6. Delivery note

When delivering to the user, include a short evaluation summary:
- final score
- weakest remaining area
- whether the document is suitable for internal strategy or external use
