# Evaluation Layer: Business Plan

Use this file to score the business-plan draft before delivery.

## 1. Purpose

Treat evaluation as a gating step, not a decorative appendix.
The business plan should be revised internally until it clears the chosen threshold.

## 2. Default threshold

- Use the user's threshold if explicitly provided.
- Otherwise default to **7/10** for internal-strategy quality.
- If the user explicitly wants an investor-facing or externally packaged version, default to **8/10**.

## 3. Evaluation modes

### Mode A: Internal strategy (default)
Prioritize:
- strategic clarity
- commercial realism
- risk honesty
- validation discipline

### Mode B: External / investor-facing
Prioritize everything in Mode A, plus:
- market framing strength
- traction evidence
- team credibility
- financial plausibility

## 4. Required scoring dimensions

Score each dimension from 1 to 10.

### A. problem and market tension
- Is the problem real, costly, and urgent?
- Is the market pull believable?

### B. product-value logic
- Does the product clearly create a result customers care about?
- Is the switching logic persuasive?

### C. commercial model quality
- Is there a real path from value to revenue?
- Are user, buyer, and payer separated where needed?

### D. GTM realism
- Is the first wedge believable?
- Is trust formation explained?
- Is expansion after the first wedge clear?

### E. pricing sanity
- Does the pricing structure match customer value?
- Are margin risks acknowledged?

### F. competition and defensibility
- Are substitutes understood?
- Is the moat more than just "AI"?

### G. risk honesty
- Are the key risks concrete and non-ceremonial?
- Are the weakest assumptions named?

### H. evidence level
- Is the plan supported by evidence appropriate to its stage?
- For external mode, is traction strong enough for the claims being made?

### I. team / founder credibility (external mode emphasized)
- Is it clear why this team can win?
- Is founder-market fit present?

### J. financial plausibility (external mode emphasized)
- Are pricing, costs, and scale assumptions at least directionally credible?

## 5. Pass / fail logic

### Pass
- overall score >= threshold
- no core dimension below 6/10

Core dimensions:
- problem and market tension
- product-value logic
- commercial model quality
- GTM realism
- risk honesty

### Conditional fail
- overall score >= threshold but one core dimension < 6/10
- revise and re-evaluate

### Fail
- overall score < threshold
- revise and re-evaluate

## 6. Revision protocol

If the draft fails:
1. identify the weakest 2-3 dimensions
2. revise the business plan specifically for them
3. re-score
4. only deliver once it passes

If it still cannot pass because evidence is missing, explicitly state:
- which dimensions are blocked
- what evidence is missing
- whether the document is currently only suitable for internal strategy

## 7. Batch comparison note

If several ideas are being compared, add a comparative score table before deep-diving.
The goal is ranking, not only pass/fail.

## 8. Delivery note

When delivering to the user, include a short evaluation summary:
- final score
- evaluation mode used
- weakest remaining area
- whether the document is suitable for internal strategy, external packaging, or neither
