# Brain Layer: Product Plan

Use this file as the main reasoning base for business-plan work.

## 1. Business plan definition

A product plan is a commercial logic document, not a long product memo.
It must explain why a product can become a business, not merely why it is interesting.

## 2. Core principles

### Principle A: Positioning before commercialization
Without a clear product thesis, the product plan will become generic.

### Principle B: Market opportunity is about buying tension, not only market size
Explain why customers will act now.

### Principle C: Revenue logic must follow value logic
Customers pay for outcomes, risk reduction, and economic gain, not for your architecture.

### Principle D: Defensibility is not the same as novelty
Look for workflow embedding, switching costs, data/context accumulation, trust, brand, and execution advantages.

### Principle E: Risk belongs in the core logic
If the biggest assumptions are hidden, the business story is weak.

## 3. Required lenses

### Lens 1: Market tension
Why does this problem matter now, and why is the timing favorable?

### Lens 2: Buyer logic
Who uses it, who influences it, who approves it, who pays for it?

### Lens 3: Value logic
What concrete result is delivered: time saved, revenue gained, risk reduced, compliance improved, etc.?

### Lens 4: Revenue logic
How does money flow: when, from whom, under what price logic?

### Lens 5: Expansion logic
How does the business grow after the first wedge?

### Lens 6: Defensibility logic
What makes the business harder to replace over time?

### Lens 7: Execution risk
What are the three assumptions most likely to break the business?

## 4. AI-specific rules

If AI is core, force answers to these:
1. Does AI increase value density or just add flair?
2. Does AI cost scale faster than willingness to pay?
3. Is quality good enough for the target workflow?
4. Does AI improve defensibility, or lower barriers for everyone?

## 5. Anti-patterns

- TAM as the main proof
- "subscription" presented as a full business model
- AI treated as moat by default
- GTM reduced to a channel list
- polite, low-information risk sections

## 6. Final rubric

Only treat the plan as mature if it is:
- commercially coherent
- stage-appropriate
- realistic about GTM
- explicit about risk
- clear on why customers will pay
