# Evaluation Layer: Product Plan

Use this file to score the product-plan draft before delivery.

## 1. Purpose

Treat evaluation as a gating step, not a decorative appendix.
The product plan should be revised internally until it clears the chosen threshold.

This evaluation is not a vibe check.
It is a structured review of whether the product plan is stable enough to become:
- a final product strategy deliverable
- a decision document for internal planning
- a credible downstream input for execution planning

## 2. Default threshold

- Use the user's threshold if explicitly provided.
- Otherwise default to **7/10** for internal-strategy quality.
- If the user explicitly wants an externally packaged version, default to **8/10**.

## 3. Evaluation modes

### Mode A: Internal strategy (default)
Prioritize:
- strategic clarity
- product and market realism
- commercial logic quality
- risk honesty
- validation discipline

### Mode B: External use
Prioritize everything in Mode A, plus:
- stronger market framing
- stronger evidence burden
- clearer credibility signals
- tighter external readability

## 4. How scoring works

Score 10 dimensions.
Each dimension gets a score from **1 to 10**.

Use the anchor points below as the primary guide:
- **1** = almost unusable
- **3** = weak / vague
- **5** = partially formed but not stable
- **7** = solid internal-strategy quality
- **9** = unusually sharp and reusable

Scores such as 6 or 8 are allowed when the draft sits between two anchors.

### Overall score

Compute the overall score as the **average of all scored dimensions**, rounded to one decimal place.

For **internal strategy mode**, score dimensions A-H as required. Dimensions I-J are optional unless clearly relevant.
For **external mode**, score A-J together.

## 5. Required scoring dimensions

### A. problem and market tension
Question: Is the problem real, costly, urgent, and backed by believable market pull?

**1**
- problem is unclear or trivial
- no believable market pull

**3**
- problem exists in theory
- urgency and market tension are vague

**5**
- problem is visible
- but not yet sharp enough to justify prioritization

**7**
- problem is concrete and commercially relevant
- the market pull is believable for the stated customer segment

**9**
- the problem is so clear and costly that urgency is obvious
- the market pull feels strong and hard to dismiss

### B. product-value logic
Question: Does the product clearly create a result customers care about?

**1**
- value logic is missing or confused
- output is feature-heavy and result-light

**3**
- some value is implied
- but the customer outcome is not sharply defined

**5**
- customer value is visible
- but switching logic is still weak or generic

**7**
- outcome is clear
- the reason to switch is mostly persuasive

**9**
- the value logic is sharp, economically meaningful, and hard to misunderstand
- the switching case is compelling

### C. commercial model quality
Question: Is there a real path from value to revenue?

**1**
- no meaningful revenue logic
- payer is unknown or incoherent

**3**
- revenue model is named
- but still generic, such as "subscription" without real structure

**5**
- charging logic exists
- but buyer, user, and payer are not yet cleanly separated

**7**
- commercial path is mostly coherent
- value and monetization are linked in a believable way

**9**
- revenue logic is clear, stage-appropriate, and tightly aligned with customer value

### D. GTM realism
Question: Is the market-entry path believable at the current stage?

**1**
- GTM is missing or fantasy-level

**3**
- GTM is mostly a channel list
- trust formation is not explained

**5**
- some plausible entry motion exists
- but the first wedge or activation logic remains soft

**7**
- first wedge is believable
- trust formation, activation, and expansion are reasonably clear

**9**
- GTM path is sharp, realistic, and well matched to the product and customer context

### E. pricing sanity
Question: Does the pricing structure match customer value and approval logic?

**1**
- pricing is missing or arbitrary

**3**
- pricing exists only as a label
- no link to value or approval logic

**5**
- pricing structure is directionally reasonable
- but the unit or packaging still feels weak

**7**
- pricing mostly matches value and customer buying behavior
- margin risks are at least acknowledged

**9**
- pricing is clear, explainable, and strategically useful
- packaging and expansion logic are strong

### F. competition and defensibility
Question: Are substitutes understood, and is the advantage more than just "AI"?

**1**
- no real substitute analysis
- no defensibility logic

**3**
- competitors are named
- but comparison is shallow and moat is vague

**5**
- substitutes are visible
- but defensibility is still partially adjective-based

**7**
- substitutes are understood
- defensibility is tied to workflow, trust, data, distribution, or switching cost

**9**
- the plan shows a strong understanding of competitive reality and a credible path to durable advantage

### G. risk honesty
Question: Are the key risks real, concrete, and decision-useful?

**1**
- risk section is missing or ceremonial

**3**
- risks are generic and non-actionable

**5**
- some real risks are named
- but weakest assumptions are still underplayed

**7**
- the main risks and weak assumptions are clearly stated
- the plan does not hide fragility behind optimism

**9**
- risk treatment is unusually honest and strategically useful
- it clearly improves decision quality

### H. evidence level
Question: Is the document supported by evidence appropriate to its stage?

**1**
- claims are mostly unsupported

**3**
- evidence is thin and mostly rhetorical

**5**
- some stage-appropriate evidence exists
- but several major claims are still under-supported

**7**
- evidence level is reasonable for the stage
- major claims are at least directionally grounded

**9**
- evidence is strong, well matched to the claims, and meaningfully reduces uncertainty

### I. team / founder credibility
Question: Is it believable that this team can execute and win?

**1**
- no credibility logic

**3**
- credibility is asserted, not shown

**5**
- some founder-market fit or execution credibility exists
- but not yet compelling

**7**
- team credibility is believable for the stated wedge and stage

**9**
- team fit is a real strategic asset, not just a resume line

### J. financial plausibility
Question: Are pricing, costs, and scale assumptions at least directionally credible?

**1**
- economics are ignored or contradictory

**3**
- economics are hand-wavy and weakly connected to the plan

**5**
- financial logic is directionally plausible
- but still rough or incomplete

**7**
- pricing, cost burden, and scale assumptions are mostly credible for the stage

**9**
- financial plausibility is unusually disciplined and reinforces the strategic story

## 6. Core dimensions and pass / fail logic

### Core dimensions
For both modes, these are core and cannot be weak:
- problem and market tension
- product-value logic
- commercial model quality
- GTM realism
- risk honesty

### Pass
- overall score >= threshold
- no core dimension below **6/10**

### Conditional fail
- overall score >= threshold
- but one or more core dimensions are below **6/10**
- revise the weak dimensions and re-evaluate

### Fail
- overall score < threshold
- revise and re-evaluate

## 7. Revision protocol

If the draft fails:
1. identify the weakest 2-3 dimensions
2. explain why they are weak in plain language
3. revise the product plan specifically for those weaknesses
4. re-score
5. only deliver once it passes

If it still cannot pass because evidence is missing, explicitly state:
- which dimensions are blocked
- what evidence is missing
- whether the document is currently only suitable for internal strategy

## 8. Batch comparison note

If several ideas are being compared, add a comparative score table before deep-diving.
The goal is ranking, not only pass/fail.

## 9. Delivery format

When delivering to the user, include a short evaluation summary with:
- final overall score
- threshold used
- evaluation mode used
- pass / conditional fail / fail
- weakest remaining area
- suitability: internal strategy / external use / not ready

Also include a compact score breakdown in this form:

```md
## 评估摘要

- 综合分：7.5 / 10
- 阈值：7 / 10
- 评估模式：内部战略版
- 结论：通过

### 维度评分
- 问题与市场张力：8/10 — 问题真实，市场张力成立，但首批场景还可再收窄
- 产品价值逻辑：8/10 — 价值清楚，但切换理由还可更像真实采购理由
- 商业模式质量：7/10 — 收费路径合理，但 buyer / payer 关系还可更清楚
- GTM 现实性：7/10 — 首批路径可行，但渠道复制性还要验证
- 定价合理性：7/10 — 定价结构成形，但价格带仍需更多实证
- 竞争与防御性：7/10 — 竞品格局清楚，但壁垒逻辑仍可更锋利
- 风险诚实度：8/10 — 风险识别较真实
- 证据水平：6/10 — 仍缺更多客户与采购验证

```

## 10. Usage note

Treat this score as a structured review result, not false precision.
The purpose is not to pretend the number is objective.
The purpose is to make evaluation consistent, debuggable, and revision-friendly.
